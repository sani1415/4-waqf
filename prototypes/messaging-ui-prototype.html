Conversation Tabs Prototype Directions

This prototype explores different ways to place a tab or mode system inside a single conversation so your app can become more message-centered without turning the thread into a messy mixed feed. Each design below keeps chat as the main thing, but organizes tasks, documents, results, and attention-needed items differently.

Single HTML/CSS prototype
Different ideas in tabs
Focused on WhatsApp-like simplicity

Design A: Simple Segmented Tabs

This is the safest version. It feels familiar, clean, and easy to understand. The conversation keeps one small segmented row under the header: All, Action Needed, and Docs.

Best for A first message-centered version of your app without making the conversation feel too experimental.
Strong point Students and teachers can quickly switch views, but still feel like they are in one chat.
Risk If you add too many tabs later, it can start looking like a mini-dashboard inside chat.

Design B: Action Needed First

In this direction, the conversation gives special importance to things that need attention. For teachers, this can be very powerful because the tab row itself highlights urgency.

Best for Teacher-heavy workflows where unread text is less important than unresolved actions.
Strong point It pushes the app beyond normal chat and makes the thread a real work center.
Risk It can feel less emotionally simple than WhatsApp if the “action” view becomes too dominant.

Design C: Floating Pills

This keeps the chat visually dominant. Instead of a full tab row, small floating pills sit above the timeline like a light filter system. It feels more modern and less “app dashboard.”

Best for If you want something elegant and lighter than normal tabs.
Strong point Keeps the conversation feeling big and immersive.
Risk Some users may not immediately realize these are important navigation modes.

Design D: Side Sheet Details

This design keeps All as the only visible conversation mode. Instead of many tabs, tapping a task or document opens a side sheet or bottom sheet for details. So the thread stays clean.

Best for If you want the strongest WhatsApp feeling while still supporting complex features.
Strong point One main conversation timeline, less visible complexity.
Risk Harder to see all documents or all tasks at a glance unless you add a secondary sheet list.

Design E: Hidden Modes Drawer

In this direction, there is no permanent row of tabs at all. A single button inside the conversation header opens a modes drawer: All, Action Needed, Docs, Tasks, Results. This keeps the main UI minimal.

Best for Maximum visual simplicity.
Strong point The conversation stays very clean and close to WhatsApp.
Risk Discoverability is lower. Users may not notice the extra modes quickly.
